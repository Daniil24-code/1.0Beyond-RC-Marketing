Section 01

Your Client Avatars

Two distinct audiences. Two completely different motivations. Every piece of content you create should be tagged to one of these two profiles before you film a single second.

Avatar 1
The Entrepreneur / Investor
US or Canadian HNW entrepreneur, age 35–60, $1M–$50M+ net worth. Built real wealth over a decade. Starting to realise a single passport is a single point of failure for everything they've built.
Zero state — where they are now
One passport. One jurisdiction. Limited international banking. Family security dependent on a single government's decisions. They've thought about this for years. They still haven't acted.
Hero state — where they want to be
A second passport or EU residency — legal, clean, fully handled. Banking across jurisdictions. Family protected regardless of geopolitical shifts. Genuinely global.
Core message
"You built the company and the wealth. We build your global freedom plan."
Avatar 2
The Introducer — Referral Partner
Wealth manager, lawyer, financial advisor, or family office professional. Already serves HNW clients. Wants to add investment migration as a value-added service without building new infrastructure.
Zero state — where they are now
Clients are already asking about second passports. No trusted referral partner. Missing passive income. Their reputation is on the line every time they point someone elsewhere without confidence.
Hero state — where they want to be
A 20% referral fee per successful client with Beyond handling everything. The simplest process imaginable: sign one agreement, make the introduction, earn when it closes.
Core message
"Your clients are already asking. Now you have the answer — and a 20% fee when it closes."
Transformation Statement — Entrepreneur / Investor
"I help high-net-worth North American entrepreneurs and investors go from holding a single passport and feeling exposed to geopolitical risk, to building a legally secure global freedom plan — so their wealth, family, and options are never dependent on a single country's decisions."
Transformation Statement — Introducer
"I help wealth managers, lawyers, and financial advisors add investment migration as a high-value, zero-overhead service — so they can answer the second passport question their HNW clients are already asking, and earn a 20% referral fee every time it closes."

△
Top of Funnel — TOF
Reach strangers. Geopolitical risk, passport myths, common mistakes. They don't know Beyond yet. Make them stop and think about something they've been ignoring.
Reach & Awareness
CTA: Follow
△

Build authority with warm viewers. Programme breakdowns, comparison content, step-by-step processes. Educate and qualify simultaneously.
Followers & Email Leads

△

Section 03

Scripted Hooks

All 12 scripted hooks adapted for Beyond's North American HNW investment migration niche. One hook per reel. The hook is your first 1–2 seconds. Never combine two hooks in the same video.

01
Bold Claim TOF
"Your passport is the most dangerous single point of failure in your entire financial life. And almost nobody treats it that way."
Reframes something they've never thought about. 'Dangerous' stops a risk-aware entrepreneur cold.
02
Mistake Hook TOF
"Stop treating your second passport as a tax scheme. That thinking is costing you the real benefit of global citizenship."
Corrects a misconception they probably hold. They keep watching to find out what the real benefit is.
03
Number Hook TOF
"3 reasons successful entrepreneurs get a second passport — and none of them are what you think."
Numbers create expectation. The 'not what you think' framing demands they verify their assumption.
04
Curiosity Gap TOF
"The real reason ultra-wealthy families hold multiple passports — and it has nothing to do with taxes."
Every entrepreneur watching has a theory. They need it confirmed or corrected. The loop must close.
05
Unpopular Opinion TOF
"Unpopular opinion: a second passport is not a luxury for the ultra-rich. It's basic risk management for any 7-figure business."
Challenges the belief that this is only for billionaires. Controversial and direct.
06
Personal Confession MOF
"I used to think global citizenship was for people hiding from their governments. Here's what changed my mind entirely."
Vulnerability with a clear before/after frame. Mirrors exactly what many first-time viewers believe.
07
Warning Hook TOF
"If your entire business wealth is tied to a single country's banking system, watch this before you continue operating that way."
Fear-based and urgent. Speaks to a real, specific financial risk that HNW viewers recognise immediately.
08
Challenge Hook TOF
"Most entrepreneurs can't answer this one question about their global financial exposure. Can you? Here it is."
Challenges their self-image as someone who has everything figured out. Ego stops the scroll.
09
Relatability Hook TOF
"POV: you've spent 10 years building a 7-figure business. You have one passport. One government can restrict your access tomorrow."
Perfectly mirrors the situation of Beyond's ideal client. They see themselves in the first five words.
10
Secret Hook TOF
"Nobody in the wealth management world tells their clients this about second passports. I'll say it plainly."
Insider framing. Positions Beyond as the advisor who tells the truth that others won't.
11
Fast Promise MOF
"In 60 seconds I'll show you every investment migration programme available to North American entrepreneurs right now — with real costs."
Entrepreneurs value their time. A precise promise earns their attention and keeps them to the end.
12
Shocking Stat TOF
"Only 1 in 10 high-net-worth North Americans holds a second passport. Here's what the other 9 are risking right now."
Creates immediate social comparison. The viewer wants to know if they're in the 1 — or the 9.
Section 04

Visual Hooks

What the viewer sees before you speak. In investment migration, the visual hook also signals premium positioning — setting, production quality, and framing establish authority before the script even starts. Pair one visual hook with one scripted hook in every reel.

📷
Mid-Action Open
Open mid-way through flipping a passport, pointing at a world map, or reviewing documents in a high-end office. No intro. No 'hey guys.' Immediate professional energy before a word is spoken.
🔤
Bold Text Overlay
Large text before any spoken word: "Your passport is a liability. Here's how to fix it." Stops the scroll on mute — how most LinkedIn-adjacent HNW audiences browse between meetings.
⚎
Passport Contrast
Left: one passport on a plain desk. Right: three passports fanned out with residency cards. The entire value proposition in one frame — no explanation needed.
🏢
Premium Setting
Film from a high-end hotel lobby, client meeting room, or financial district backdrop. HNW viewers respond to peer-level environments. Premium setting signals premium service.
👁
Close-Up Face
Extreme close-up, direct eye contact, serious expression. Hold one full second before speaking. Creates the feeling of a direct, private, one-to-one conversation.
🗺
Point at Map
Point at a world map highlighting Caribbean or European locations, or at a cost breakdown on screen. Combines movement, visual data, and eye direction — three stimuli in one second.
🌎
Before / After Contrast Cut
Hard cut: one country highlighted on a world map, then 30+ countries lit up. The entire value proposition communicated in under two seconds — no narration needed.
👑
Passport Stack Reveal
Start with a closed hand. Open it to reveal multiple passports. No context. Pure curiosity. "What is that?" is one of the most powerful scroll-stopping triggers on any platform.
✈
Slow-Motion Travel
Cinematic slow-motion of a premium location — European city street, Caribbean shoreline, private airport. Aspirational visuals connect the product to a lifestyle outcome the viewer already wants.
⏳
Timer / Countdown
"You have 90 seconds to understand your biggest financial blind spot." Timer visible from frame one. Time pressure is primal. Entrepreneurs hate wasted seconds — a countdown earns them.
🔍
Document Zoom-In
Wide shot of a passport on a desk, then rapid zoom into the country seal and name. Movement directs attention. Creates a "what country is that?" moment that demands a full watch.
⚡
Caption Mismatch
Footage of a standard passport control queue with text: "How my clients skip this in 47 countries." Cognitive dissonance stops the brain cold — it needs to resolve the contradiction.

Complete CTA Library

One CTA per video — always. Every extra CTA you add reduces the chance of any being followed. Pick the one action you most want the viewer to take, match it to the funnel stage, and ask for that only.

△
Follower CTAs
Grow your audience — use on TOF content targeting strangers who have never seen Beyond before.
TOF
The Part 2 Hook
"Follow so you don't miss Part 2 — I'm covering the exact process for getting a Caribbean passport as a US or Canadian citizen."
Creates urgency. The follow becomes the price of admission for what's already promised.
The Series Promise
"Follow — I post investment migration breakdowns for North American entrepreneurs every week."
Niche-specific language filters for the right audience immediately. People follow when they know exactly what's coming.
The Value Exchange
"Follow if you want to understand your actual global options — not the sales version, the real one."
Addresses the distrust HNW investors feel toward this industry. Frames the follow as access to honesty.
The Identity CTA
"If you're serious about protecting what you've built — not just managing it — follow."
Builders who are 'serious' act. Those who aren't scroll. That's the right filter for a premium advisory audience.
The Content Promise
"Follow — my next video covers the one Caribbean programme that gives US entrepreneurs E-2 treaty access. Most advisors don't mention it."
Specific insider knowledge teased. Highest-value follow incentive in this niche.
The Repeat Reminder
"I post breakdowns like this every Tuesday and Friday — follow so they land in your feed."
Specific schedule builds habit. Followers who expect you show up. Showing up builds trust before any consultation.
△
DM CTAs
Start advisory conversations — use on MOF content with warm viewers who already know Beyond.
MOF
The Keyword DM
"DM me the word 'PASSPORT' and I'll send you the full programme comparison — Caribbean and European options side by side, with real costs."
One word to type. The specific deliverable makes it a genuine value exchange, not a funnel.
The Question DM
"DM me your biggest question about second citizenship right now — I personally answer every one."
Invites a real conversation. When you respond thoughtfully, trust rises faster than any content piece ever could.
The Free Resource DM
"DM me 'COMPARE' and I'll send you the programme comparison sheet I use with every new client."
Specific, useful, zero risk. The keyword makes it trackable and the resource makes the DM worth initiating.
The Honest Offer
"If you've been thinking about a second passport for your family and want a straight answer — DM me."
Sounds like a real conversation, not a funnel. HNW clients respond to honesty and avoid pressure.
The Vulnerability DM
"If you've been putting this off for years and aren't sure where to start — DM me. No agenda, just a conversation."
Removes the pressure entirely. Many HNW clients have been thinking about this for years without acting.
The Behind the Scenes
"DM me 'INSIDE' and I'll show you what a full Caribbean CBI application actually looks like from start to approval."
Exclusive access in a space where most information is locked behind consultation fees.

Lead CTAs
Convert to consultations and introductions — use on BOF content with your most engaged, loyal audience.
BOF
The Free Consultation
"DM me 'PLAN' if you'd like a free 20-minute call to talk through which programme makes sense for your situation."
Zero risk. The free call qualifies the lead and starts a genuine advisory relationship.
The Social Proof Lead
"We've completed hundreds of successful applications across Europe and the Caribbean. If you're ready to start yours — DM me."
20+ years of proven track record is the most powerful thing Beyond has. Use it directly as the conversion mechanism.

Self-qualifying at the highest level. Only serious warm leads respond. High quality over high quantity.

Proof + geography + emotional keyword. 'Freedom' is the deepest desire in the avatar profile.

Real scarcity signals a premium service. Beyond isn't chasing anyone. That positioning matters with HNW clients.

"If you're a wealth manager or lawyer whose clients are asking about second passports — DM me 'PARTNER.' I'll walk you through the referral programme in 15 minutes."
Dedicated CTA for the Introducer avatar. Speaks their language. Highly specific conversion trigger.
Section 07

Story Selling Scripts

All 10 scripts follow the same 5-part structure. Never lead with the product — lead with the problem the product solves. In investment migration, trust is built through specificity, honesty, and documented proof.

The 5-Part Structure
PartWhat it does
Part 1 — The problemOpen with a geopolitical, financial, or freedom risk the viewer feels personally. If they don't see their situation in Part 1, they won't stay for the solution.
Part 2 — Who you areIntroduce Beyond through the lens of the problem. 20+ years of experience matters because of what it means for the viewer's outcome — not as a credential to be proud of.
Part 3 — The solutionBe specific. The more specific the explanation, the more credible you appear. Vague advice feels like marketing. Specific advice feels like expertise.
Part 4 — The resultOne measurable outcome. Not a category — one specific result. A client approved in 9 months. A family protected. A European residency secured.
Part 5 — The CTAOne clear action. Follow for TOF. DM keyword for MOF. Consultation DM or link in bio for BOF.

Build before you sell
Post at least 4 weeks of TOF + MOF content before any BOF lead-generation CTAs. Build the audience first. Applications follow naturally once trust is established.
Be specific, not vague
Programme-specific content with real costs and timelines massively outperforms vague 'global freedom' content. Name the country, the investment amount, the timeline. Specificity signals expertise.
The double down rule
When geopolitical risk content spikes in performance — and it will during news events — film the follow-up variation within 48 hours. The algorithm is warm and already knows who to show it to.
Two audiences, two tracks
The Entrepreneur/Investor and the Introducer need separate content with separate CTAs. Saturday is locked for Introducers. Don't blend the messaging — each avatar has a completely different motivation.
